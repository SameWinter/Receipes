Chowmein

The street food culture in India is very vibrant with diverse variety of street food in every state. From pani puri, to pav bhaji to momos, Indian streets are always filled with a dearth of delectable and spicy street foods and chowmein is one of them. A lip-smacking and mouthwatering classic Indo-Chinese Street food which has a fan base in every corner of the country, Chow Mein is a scrumptious street food made from long and thin noodles, sauteed vegetables, sauces, and aromatics. It is a masterpiece of long and velvety noodles with a rustic taste, made even more divine by sauteed veggies and lip-smacking sauces that makes it highly tempting and utterly slurp-able to the last bite.

Receipe

Noodles, vegetables, vinegar, Sauces and spices.

  1. In a large pot add 2L water, salt and heat it, and bring it to a bubbling boil add 2 packs of Noodles and cook it for a while
  2. Once the Noodles are cooked, transfer them to a strainer to drain the water and then apply oil to the Noodles and keep it aside
  3. For the chowmein sauce, in a bowl add 1 tbsp vinegar, 1 tsp Red Chilli Sauce, 1 tsp Green CHilli Sauce, 1 tsp Soya Sauce and ½ tsp powdered sigar and mix everything. keep aside
  4. For the powdered spices, add ½ garam masala, ¼ Degi Red Chilli Powder, and salt to taste, mix everything and keep aside.
  5. Now in a oan heat 2 tbsp oil and add 2 sliced onions, 5-6 vhopped garlic, 1 inch chopped ginger and 3 chopped green chillies and sauté for 1 minute
  6. Now add boiled noodles, the sauces mixtures prepared earlier, powdered spices mixture, ¼ tsp Soya Sauce, ½ tsp Red Chilli Sauce and toss everything properly
  7. Chow Mein is ready to serve.
